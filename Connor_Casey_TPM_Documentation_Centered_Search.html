- Activate the Machine Lock and Auxillary Function Lock functions on the main operating panel.

- Turn Off the "Mode Unlock" key switch on the main operation panel (only for Door Lock (power Out system) (option)).
- Open the operator door.
- Confirm that the [TOOL UNCLAMP] switch is Off (the spindle is clamped).
- Screw the T-wrench into the tapped hole in the end of the sealing rod.
- After strongly screwing in the T-wrench, turn 90 degrees CW while the spindle is locked. (the hook is released, and the sealing rod is pushed out by the internal spring.)

- Press the [TOOL UNCLAMP] switch while the T-wrench is mounted, and then remove the sealing rod.

- Apply thread-locking adhesive (medium strength) to the threads on the inner sleeve.
- Attach the inner sleeve and rubber seal to the sealing rod (tightening torque: 20 Nm).
- Confirm that the rubber seal projects evenly by 0.7+0.1mm.
- Confirm that the spindle is unclamped.
- Align the hook and sealing rod groove, and return the sealing rod to the original position.
- Check that the T-wrench does not turn in the CW direction.
- Press the [TOOL UNCLAMP] switch again to clamp the spindle.
- Press in the T-wrench while the spindle is locked in place, and turn 90 degrees CW.
- Check that the T-wrench does not turn in the CW direction while the spindle is locked in place.

- Clamp the through-spindle coolant tool in the spindle and confirm that through-spindle coolant is discharged correctly.

- Confirm that there is no cutting fluid leakage inside the spindle taper.
